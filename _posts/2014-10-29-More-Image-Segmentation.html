## Refining the Segmentation Inputs To eleminate some of the noise from the segmentation analysis, we had to reduce the amount of image that went was input into the segmentation algorithm. We used our [time derivative analysis](http://jallen30gt.github.io/MIC-Tungsten-Nanowires/2014/10/14/Time-Evolution.html) to aid in this decision. We also have an abberation in our images that affects our segmentation analysis as well. Using the time derivative analysis, we decided on running our segmentation analysis on columns 400 - 700 of each of the images. To reduce the noise in data due to the abberation, we decided on further limiting each image to rows 200 - 500.
