Step 1: We a grayscale image to work on (Call it A)
![Original Image A](http://i.imgur.com/s5e62wp.jpg)

Step 2: We use [IMADJUST](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imadjust.html) on the image (Call it B)
![IMADJUST of Image A (B)](http://i.imgur.com/2xbaLjf.jpg)

Step 3: We use [IMCOMPLEMENT](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imcomplement.html) on image A (Call it C)
![Complement of Image A (C)](http://i.imgur.com/suVMUY9.jpg)

Step 4: We use [IMADJUST](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imadjust.html) on the image B (Call it D)
![IMADJUST of Complement of Image A (D)](http://i.imgur.com/QmdoM4B.jpg)

Step 5: We use subtract image D from Image B (Call it E)
![Subtraction of Image D from Image B (E)](http://i.imgur.com/mV71tD8.jpg)

Step 6: We use [IMCOMPLEMENT](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imcomplement.html) on image E (Call it F)
![Complement of Image E (F)](http://i.imgur.com/661Hhqy.jpg)

Notes: * You can play with thresholding values to decrease or increase mid-range pixel values * You can use [IMDILATE](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imdilate.html) or [IMERODE](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imerode.html) to thicken or thin lines * You can use in tandem with Canny Edge Detection software to pick up some of the edges that the Canny Edge Detection missed. * Seems to work really well with [image derivatives](http://www.mathworks.com/help/images/ref/imgradient.html)
